Malaysia’s Tourism Comeback: Who Returned, Where They Entered, and Which Places Benefited?

Malaysia’s international tourism story did not simply restart after the pandemic. It rebuilt itself through different countries, border gateways, airports, seaports, and states. This visualisation follows that recovery from 2020 to 2024 and shows how the comeback was shaped by geography.

A data story using arrivals by country, state of entry, point of entry, gender, and location.

Section 01

The Recovery Curve

The first view shows the overall return of international arrivals to Malaysia. The line makes the disruption clear: arrivals dropped sharply in 2020, stayed low during the restricted travel period, and then rose again as travel reopened.

Key idea: Malaysia’s tourism recovery was not gradual at first. The comeback became visible only after arrivals began rising strongly from 2022 onward.
